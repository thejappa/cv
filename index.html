Thejappa Golla

Associate Research Scientist

I specialize in the observational and theoretical studies of radio phenomena originating from the solar atmosphere and interplanetary medium. I have authored or co-authored more than 60 scientific papers. I have extensively used the Ulysses, Wind and STEREO high time resolution observations of in situ waves to study the nonlinear processes in the solar wind. I have developed statistical ray tracing programs and higher order spectral analysis techniques to study the radio emissions and in situ waves associated beam-plasma and collision-less shock systems.
